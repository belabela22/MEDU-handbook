Department Position Guide

Medical Education Developments
The development role will attain feedback and suggestions from staff members and admins. They will work to assess and innovate new developments that could benefit the academic standing of Southwest Medicine.

Medical Education Coordinator
This executive role will aim to manage the internal staff within the Medical Education Department. They will coordinate the calendar, avoid conflicts, and work with developments to create the most efficient system possible.

Medical Operations Supervisor
The Medical Operations supervisor will overview SWM‚Äôs medical staff by supervising cases and noting down common mistakes they see. They will be working with our HR department by recommending staff who should be rewarded or disciplined.

Medical Education Manager
The Medical Education Manager will facilitate the educational system, ensuring a smooth approach. They will manage the trello, recommend educators and interns who deserve validation, and manage training. They will look to be approved by the coordinator and will work directly with coordination staff.

Medical Educator
Medical Educators will have passed their Medical Education Internship and will focus on providing and creating training. Any training created will be approved before they are published. These staff will work with interns, introducing them to the world of medical education.

Medical Educator Intern
These staff are currently learning their way around educating staff. They will work with certified educators, assisting them and learning the mechanics of teaching. They will work towards the Medical Educator role by completing training, approved by the Overseer and Coordinator.

ü©∫ Section 5: Weekly Task Quota

In order to develop the very vast department of medical education, it is crucial that everyone in our team contributes. The following tasks listed will be our main focuses:

  • Task 1: Trivia Cards
    Your assignment this week is to make 10 detailed trivia cards. They do not have to be a specific specialty, so you may choose that on your own. They must have 10 questions, and an answer guide for whoever chooses to use them. They may be used for trivia night, so make sure they are neat and presentable.
  • Task 2: Research Projects
    Members will be asked to do research on a topic that they desire and then create a guide about the topic along with a quiz and or guide. You can use Canva or Google Docs, and if you have any other website you would like to use, reach out to the Overseers!
  • Task 3: Ethical Case Discussion
    Members will be asked to get into groups of two. They will create a group name and be added into a ticket. One of the overseers will provide a realistic and real-life study. They will be asked to try and solve the case. This task develops critical thinking and ethical reasoning skills in a supportive environment. This will be a fun and competitive task against their peers. You and your partner will be expected to complete this in a timely manner, and to work together! If there is any issues with this feel free to contact me or Midnight, and it will be solved!
  • Task 4: Advanced Research Methodology
    Members will be asked to research on a topic/specialty that they are not familiar with and will be required to do qualitative research, biostatistics, and grant writing. The goal for this task is to equip members with the skills needed to conduct high-quality research and secure funding. You will each be assigned your topic, when you are assigned this task! Which means you are expected to ping either me or Midnight, to get assigned a research topic!
  • Task 5: Advanced Case Study
    Create realistic and detailed case studies on various medical conditions, incorporating real-world scenarios, patient histories, and treatment plans. If you need to use any resources, please list them in your document. If you need a format, feel free to ping me and I can provide one!
  • Task 6: Detailed Staff Report
    Staff reviews will be frequent. As you moderate the server, you will make an effort to write a report about a player. This report will include the date, the type of case, specialty, and an overall summary of their performance. This will be used to assess which players should be rewarded, and which players deserve demotion. This requires you to be in-game, in order to observe cases. It should be strictly medical wise, only. You can choose any case you would like, and then write your report on it.
  • Task 7: Continuous Professional Development
    Create a series of CPD courses for practicing clinicians, focusing on emerging medical knowledge, new treatment protocols, and ongoing skill development. This will ensure that healthcare providers stay current with the latest updates in medicine throughout their careers. CPD: Continuous professional development (CPD) is a process that helps professionals maintain and improve their skills, knowledge, and experience. It involves tracking and documenting what professionals learn and experience both formally and informally, and then applying that knowledge. CPD can help professionals feel more competent, advance their careers, etc.

ü©∫ Section 11: Medical Education Internship

In order to advance from a Medical Educator Intern ‚Üí Medical Educator, you must complete your MEDU internship. The internship has 2 components. The first section will be a mandatory training. This training will cover the following:

  • Any information in the handbook
  • Training manners and professionalism
  • How to find reliable sources (I will also offer some great resources!)
  • Common FAQs!

The second part of the internship will be the final task. This final task may be submitted after your training. The final task will be to create TWO training guides/lectures on a topic of your choice. Some common examples include: IV set-up, arterial lines, bedside manner, or a procedure/disease of your choice.

Passing both the training and the final task will allow you to further get promoted. Failure to complete these trainings will prohibit you from getting promoted within the MEDU department.

What happens if I fail my training/final task?

Failure of the first training will require you to wait 5 days before re-doing this process. During these 5 days, it is highly recommended to review your failed work and see what you could do differently. Failure of this process the second time will result in demotion/expulsion from the department.
